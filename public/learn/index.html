FAQs about running a hostel in Colombia

HostelWorld, Booking and other OTAs
What fees do OTAs charge?

HostelWorld charge 15% of all bookings made at your hostel. They also charge an additional 3% for page placement benefits. Booking charge 12.5% and Expedia charge 12%.

Do I need to use OTAs?

While OTAs are very expensive, they are the primary way that travellers book their accommodation. Hostels with a large presence can attract customers directly to their website and avoid commissions but this is very difficult for a new hostel.

Payments
How to accept credit cards in Colombia?

There are a few different options. It is possible to get a point of sale (POS) from local banks such as Davivienda and Bancolombia. However, the process is time consuming, very difficult if you're not Colombian and whilie the fees vary, they're usually expensive. Tab is a US start-up that operates in Colombia. They help tourist businesses take payments online, in-person and charge no-shows. They charge a 2.9% fee to the business. PayPal is an option for online payments and they're obviously well known. However, their fees are 8.9% + $0.30 when you factor in currency conversion in Colombia.

Making and saving money
How can I make extra money in my hostel?

Offering tours or partnering with local providers can be a great way to make extra money. You can also offer food at the hostel - an opt-in dinner is a great way to create a community too. Pub crawls, airport transfers and laundry are also ways of making extra revenue.

Furniture
Thoughts on triple bunk beds?

They can be a good way of saving money but you risk getting bad reviews from travellers that find them claustraphobic. Also, it could be a security concern.
